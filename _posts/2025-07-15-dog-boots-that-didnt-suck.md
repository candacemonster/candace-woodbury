*finally. boots that stayed on.*

there are two kinds of dogs: the ones who tolerate gear and the ones who launch a boot into orbit within thirty seconds. mine are in the second category, with enthusiasm.

### the actual problem

- hot pavement and sharp rock.  
- one dog who sheds footwear like it's a side hustle.  
- me, tired of watching money bounce down the trail.

we’ve tried a lot of boots. most made it a quarter mile before they bailed.

### what finally worked

**[dog mocs](https://www.dogmocs.com/)**  
- stayed on (apparently this is not standard).  
- decent grip without that clumsy “moon boot” vibe.  
- flexible enough that they didn’t walk like tiny malfunctioning robots.  
- held up on rock and heat better than anything else we’ve tried.

not perfect—nothing is—but they didn’t become expensive trail litter, which feels like growth.

### things i noticed

- the strap system matters more than the boot itself.  
- sizing is actually accurate; measuring paws is awkward but worth it.  
- they still look at me like i’ve personally betrayed them every time i put the boots on.

### what i’ll do differently next time

- do more of: putting them on anyway and going farther without worrying about paws.  
- do less of: buying boots because the colors are cute.  
- try next: thin socks underneath for the drama‑queen paws, if only for the emotional support.

field note filed: boots stayed on, dogs are fine, and i’m slightly less suspicious of dog gear now.
