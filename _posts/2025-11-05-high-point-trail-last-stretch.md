*sometimes the last hike looks like an ordinary morning.*

location: high point trail, red cliffs desert reserve, st. george, utah.  
conditions: off cottonwood springs rd / old dump rd (black gulch trailhead).  
energy level: heavy. sore hearts.

we checked out a few trails the morning of october 25th. nothing urgent, just wandering, letting the girls stretch their legs. the plan was a quick scout at the trailhead—see what’s what, decide what to come back to for a longer hike or bike ride when joints and attitudes felt up for it.

old age and spinal arthritis had other plans. they showed up fast and mean a few days later. all the casual “next time” talk evaporated, and suddenly we were saying goodbye, for good, to our baby and trail partner, tove.

desert light has looked different ever since.

### what i noticed

the trail itself was quiet, so the distractions had to work harder:

- yellow flashes — desert marigold, probably, stubborn and loud.  
- long, dancing grasses — moving like they knew someone was watching.  
- purple asters — little disco accents pushing through rosette leaves.  
- orange globemallow — extroverts, clearly.  
- low, white desert spurge — hugging the rocks, probably the smartest of all of us.

### terrain notes

sandy, rocky, mostly flat. not much elevation gain on paper, but every bump counts when your pack is older and every joint has a history. dog approval rating was high for our girls, apart from the holes—existential and otherwise.

### for fellow wanderers

- bring water. stay humble. desert trails do not care about your backstory.  
- if you’re here with old dogs, take extra photos you don’t think you need.  
- leave no trace except maybe a few quiet memories that will ambush you later.
